# ESPN Team Abbreviations Reference

This page lists the team abbreviations used by the ESPN API, organized by league. Enter these values (comma-separated) in the **Favorite Teams** field on the Sports Ticker settings page to filter the ticker to only games involving those teams.

Abbreviations are **case-insensitive** — `LAL`, `lal`, and `Lal` all work.

> **Note on UCL:** Champions League participants change each season. If a team you follow is not shown, try their abbreviation from their domestic league (e.g. `ARS` for Arsenal, `RMA` for Real Madrid).
>
> **Note on NCAA leagues:** There are hundreds of college teams. Use your school's standard ESPN abbreviation (e.g. `MICH`, `OSU`, `UNC`, `DUKE`, `ARIZ`). If you're unsure, look up a recent ESPN scoreboard for your school to find the exact abbreviation used.
>
> **Note on NASCAR and Tennis:** These are individual-sport leagues. The Favorite Teams filter does not apply — NASCAR and ATP/WTA results are not filtered by favorites.

## NASCAR

NASCAR results display driver names and positions, not team abbreviations. The **Favorite Teams** field does not filter NASCAR results — all top-5 running positions are always shown during a live race.

---

## Tennis (ATP / WTA)

Tennis scores display player names, not team abbreviations. The **Favorite Teams** field does not apply to ATP or WTA results.

---

## NCAA Leagues

NCAA leagues (NCAAF, NCAAB, NCAA Baseball, NCAA Softball, NCAA Volleyball, NCAA Lacrosse) have hundreds of programs. Use your school's standard ESPN abbreviation. If you're unsure of the exact abbreviation, check a recent ESPN scoreboard page for your school — the abbreviation ESPN displays is what the ticker uses.

Common examples: `MICH`, `OSU`, `UNC`, `DUKE`, `ARIZ`, `UCLA`, `USC`, `TENN`, `KU`, `UK`, `ND`, `PSU`, `UF`, `GT`, `VT`, `LSU`, `AUB`, `BAMA`, `MSU`
